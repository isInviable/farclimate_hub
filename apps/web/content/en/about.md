## A collective action ladder for climate adaptation

The FARCLIMATE Transformation Hub was shaped through strategic research and co-design with primary users. It is built on three design principles: **user-centred co-design** that prioritises practitioners over generic repositories; an **agile, iterative process** that validates features with real users; and **open, interoperable solutions** that connect with other European knowledge platforms.

Conceptually, the Hub works as a **collective action ladder** with three interconnected roles:

- **Repository** — curated climate adaptation content grounded in FAIR data principles (findable, accessible, interoperable, reusable).
- **Consultation tools** — search, filters, maps, and structured “recipes” to explore and compare solutions.
- **Interaction and decision support** — saving, sharing, and recombining knowledge for local adaptation planning.

Rather than becoming another passive archive, the Hub deliberately focuses on **meaningful engagement and sustained learning** that helps users implement adaptation in their territories.

## Bioregions, communities, and sectors

The platform is anchored in three starting points: the **bioregion** as a meaningful spatial scale where ecology, economy, and governance meet; the **community** as the primary audience; and **climate adaptation solutions** tested or proposed in real contexts.

Primary users include people responsible at local and regional levels in public institutions, economic sectors, or civil society. Secondary users include citizens, and technical or political representatives at regional, national, or European levels. Scientists participate either as policy supporters or as partners in territorial alliances developing context-specific solutions.

The Hub prioritises **agriculture, forestry, and fisheries** — the three economic sectors at the heart of FARCLIMATE — while organising content by geography, sector, hazards, and keywords to close gaps found on platforms such as Climate-ADAPT.

## Inspiration, learning, and the “recipe”

Past experience with adaptation platforms showed that social-network features alone rarely sustain active communities. The Transformation Hub therefore emphasises **inspiration and learning from applied experiences** — including successes and limitations — presented in formats that practitioners can understand, compare, and transfer.

The **recipe** metaphor guides how solutions are described: like cooking, adaptation requires adapting proven “recipes” to local ingredients and conditions. Each recipe structures a case around sector, geography, hazards, adaptation type, context, objectives, implementation, outcomes, lessons learnt, transferability, and contacts — aligned with Climate-ADAPT and enhanced through FARCLIMATE user research.

Example prototyping cases included fisheries (ciguatera control in the Canary Islands), agriculture (flood water storage in the Middle Tisza, Hungary), and forestry (climate-adapted tree species in Hesse, Germany).

## Platform components

Key content and features include:

- **Curated portfolio of adaptation solutions** drawn from European and international sources, harmonised through a semantic framework and enriched where possible with geolocation, timelines, and multilingual access.
- **Connected Action for EU Adaptation** — a focused visualisation of actors providing data, tools, technical assistance, and finance (without overwhelming users with exhaustive lists).
- **Introductory pathways** on adaptation concepts, hazards, and impacts.
- **Skills** — curated e-learning, webinars, and toolkits from trusted providers.
- **Inspiring stories** — narrative content highlighting leadership in climate action, including women shaping adaptation efforts.
- **AI-assisted exploration** — embedded transparently in search, summarisation, and comparison tasks, aligned with EU digital and AI governance principles.

## How users move through the Hub

The user journey was refined with stakeholders into three stages:

1. **Search & explore** — broad search with filters for bioregion, sector, hazards, and project characteristics.
2. **Analysis & assessment** — maps, timelines, comparisons, and AI-assisted summaries across multiple solutions.
3. **Information management & collaboration** — saving selections, producing shareable outputs (reports, podcasts, slides), and pinboards for teamwork.

Co-design workshops (May 2025, Soria) and follow-up interviews with forestry, fisheries, and agriculture practitioners confirmed demand for contextual relevance, visual trust signals, comparison tools, multilingual content, and export formats suited to both digital and in-person collaboration.

## Built on evidence, designed with users

The Hub integrates knowledge from platforms such as [Climate-ADAPT](https://climate-adapt.eea.europa.eu/en), national case-study catalogues across Europe, EU Mission and Horizon projects, and scientific assessments — while addressing known gaps (sectoral balance, cost information, and lessons from unsuccessful approaches).

The first operational prototype consolidated case studies primarily from Climate-ADAPT, complemented where needed (for example fisheries content from CAKE), and validated navigation, filters, visualisations, and sharing workflows through iterative prototyping and co-creation. The present platform advances that foundation as the FARCLIMATE Transformation Hub: **a catalyst for knowledge-driven climate adaptation in Europe’s bioregions**.
